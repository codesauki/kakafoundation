========================================================
  KOWA NAMU NE FOUNDATION
  IMPACT REPORT — EDUCATION, YOUTH & COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT
  January – December 2025 | Kaduna State
========================================================

A community's greatest investment is in its people — especially its
children and youth. In 2025, the Kowa Namu Ne Foundation made bold,
lasting investments in education, youth leadership, civic engagement,
and community infrastructure across Kaduna North. From commissioning a
full school for orphans to installing solar street lights that now light
up the night across 40+ locations, this was a year of building Kaduna
from the ground up — brick by brick, child by child.


--------------------------------------------------------
  OUR APPROACH
--------------------------------------------------------

Education and community development under the Kowa Namu Ne philosophy
are not charity — they are investments in the future. Every school bag
distributed, every child returned to school, every street light
installed carries a deliberate message: the government sees you, your
community matters, and your future is worth fighting for.


--------------------------------------------------------
  KEY ACTIVITIES & OUTCOMES — EDUCATION
--------------------------------------------------------

COMMISSIONING OF KOWA NAMU NE FOUNDATION SCHOOL — April 26th
The single most significant educational milestone of 2025: a fully
built, furnished, and maintained school for orphans and less privileged
children was commissioned under the Kowa Namu Ne Foundation. The school
offers free Islamic and Western education, ensuring that children who
have lost parents or grown up in poverty are not robbed of their right
to learn. This institution stands as a lasting legacy of the
Foundation's commitment to every child in Kaduna North.

SKILLS ACQUISITION GRADUATION CEREMONY — August 30th
Over 300 trainees from diverse backgrounds successfully completed
vocational skills programs under the Foundation. The graduation
ceremony at Hamdala Hotel, Muhd Buhari Way, celebrated these achievers
and marked a significant milestone in human capital development.
Graduates walked away with practical, market-ready skills to build
independent livelihoods.

DISTRIBUTION OF BOOKS & SCHOOL BAGS — September 29th
Educational materials — including textbooks and school bags — were
distributed to students in public schools across Kaduna North. This
initiative directly reduced the financial burden on families and ensured
that lack of learning materials would not become a barrier to education.

BACK-TO-SCHOOL SUPPORT — November 11th & 12th
Uniforms, school bags, and writing materials were provided to students
to support their return to school for the new academic term. The
programme reached hundreds of children across multiple communities,
ensuring they resumed class fully equipped and ready to learn.

TOTAL SCHOOL MATERIALS DISTRIBUTED: 10,000+
Across all educational initiatives in 2025, over 10,000 units of school
materials — books, bags, uniforms, and stationery — were distributed
to students in Kaduna North.

STUDENTS RETURNED TO SCHOOL: 200
Through targeted re-enrollment support, 200 out-of-school children were
identified and returned to active learning — each one representing a
future reclaimed.


--------------------------------------------------------
  KEY ACTIVITIES & OUTCOMES — YOUTH DEVELOPMENT
--------------------------------------------------------

YOUTH ENGAGEMENT ACROSS KADUNA NORTH — March 18th
Hon. Kaka held structured engagements with youth groups across Kaduna
North, encouraging political participation, leadership consciousness,
and active involvement in entrepreneurship and community development.
This was not a political rally — it was a leadership conversation,
placing young people at the centre of Kaduna's future.

SUPPORT FOR KADPOLY LEADERSHIP SUMMIT — July 29th
A financial contribution was made to the Kaduna Polytechnic Leadership
Summit, supporting youth-led intellectual engagement, academic
development, and leadership grooming among tertiary students.

STARTUP CAPITAL FOR YOUNG ENTREPRENEURS — June & November
Two batches of young Team Kaka members received startup capital to
launch and sustain their own businesses, building a generation of
self-reliant entrepreneurs who no longer wait for jobs but create them.

SUPPORT AT YOUNG ENTREPRENEURS SUMMIT — September 27th
Financial assistance was provided to young SME owners at the Young
Entrepreneurs Summit, reinforcing the Foundation's belief that
innovation and enterprise are the fastest routes to youth independence.

SPONSORSHIP OF INEC VOTER REGISTRATION — September to November
Thousands of Kaduna North residents — many of them first-time voters —
were supported to complete their online INEC voter registration, with
Team Kaka deploying volunteers and devices across communities. This
initiative directly strengthened democratic participation and gave
young people a formal voice in governance.

REGISTRATIONS FACILITATED: 12,000+
Over twelve thousand voter registrations were processed, making this
one of the largest civic mobilisation drives in Kaduna North in 2025.


--------------------------------------------------------
  KEY ACTIVITIES & OUTCOMES — COMMUNITY INFRASTRUCTURE
--------------------------------------------------------

SOLAR INSTALLATION — Masallacin Malam Goma — May 1st
Solar power was installed at the mosque to improve lighting, safety,
and electricity access for worshippers and surrounding residents —
turning a place of worship into a beacon of light for the community.

SOLAR PROJECT — Shehu Laminu Road, U/Rimi — May 13th
A solar project was delivered to enhance community safety, improve
night-time visibility, and promote general well-being for residents
along this key community artery.

SOLAR STREET LIGHTS ACROSS MULTIPLE WARDS — November 6th
Solar-powered street lights were installed across communities in
multiple wards, transforming the safety, security, and quality of life
for thousands of residents. Marketwomen can now trade later, children
can study after dark, and communities are safer at night.

TOTAL SOLAR & STREET LIGHT INSTALLATIONS: 40+ locations
