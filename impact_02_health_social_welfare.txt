========================================================
  KOWA NAMU NE FOUNDATION
  IMPACT REPORT — HEALTH & SOCIAL WELFARE
  January – December 2025 | Kaduna State
========================================================

Access to healthcare is not a privilege — it is a right. In 2025, the
Kowa Namu Ne Foundation made healthcare accessible to hundreds of
residents across Kaduna North who would otherwise have gone without
treatment, medication, or insurance. Through free medical outreaches,
eye screenings, hospital bill settlements, and health insurance
sponsorships, Hon. Abdulazeez Abubakar Kaka brought quality care
directly to the doorsteps of the people.


--------------------------------------------------------
  OUR APPROACH
--------------------------------------------------------

The Foundation's health interventions are built on a simple belief:
no resident of Kaduna North should suffer or die because they cannot
afford care. Every outreach, every sponsored insurance enrollment, every
settled hospital bill is an act of restoring dignity to people who
deserve better.


--------------------------------------------------------
  KEY ACTIVITIES & OUTCOMES
--------------------------------------------------------

FREE MEDICAL OUTREACH — June 15th, Malali
Hundreds of residents received free medical consultations, medications,
and health education at a large-scale outreach in the Malali community.
Trained healthcare workers were deployed to screen, diagnose, and treat
patients on the spot, with medications distributed at no cost.

MEDICAL OUTREACH & EYE SCREENING — August 2nd, Kabala Ward
A comprehensive medical outreach was held in Kabala Ward, combining
general healthcare services with specialized eye screening. Free
eyeglasses were distributed to residents with vision impairments,
many of whom had gone years without proper optical care.

EYE GLASSES DISTRIBUTED: 300 beneficiaries received free eyeglasses
across outreaches, restoring vision and quality of life for individuals
who had been unable to afford corrective lenses.

HEALTH INSURANCE SPONSORSHIP (KADCHMA) — July 28th
In a landmark intervention, Hon. Kaka sponsored over 100 Kaduna North
residents into the Kaduna State Contributory Health Management Authority
(KADCHMA) scheme — providing them with one full year of health
insurance coverage. This initiative gave low-income residents access to
formal healthcare services at little or no personal cost.

SETTLEMENT OF MEDICAL BILLS — October 13th, Primary Health Centres
Outstanding hospital bills of over 60 patients at primary healthcare
centres were settled by the Foundation, ensuring that those who had
already received care were not detained or denied further treatment
due to financial inability. No one was turned away.

ENGAGEMENT WITH PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES — July 6th
Hon. Kaka held a structured engagement with persons with disabilities
and Kauraye groups, providing financial support to promote inclusion,
dignity, and economic participation. This initiative recognized the
compounded vulnerability faced by persons with disabilities and took
concrete steps to address it.

FOOD DISTRIBUTION TO VULNERABLE FAMILIES — March 19th
Food items were distributed to over 500 low-income households, providing
nutritional relief and reinforcing social welfare for families facing
economic hardship — a direct response to food insecurity at the
household level.
