========================================================
  KOWA NAMU NE FOUNDATION
  IMPACT REPORT — ECONOMIC EMPOWERMENT & LIVELIHOODS
  January – December 2025 | Kaduna State
========================================================

At the Kowa Namu Ne Foundation, we believe that sustainable communities
are built on economic dignity. In 2025, Hon. Abdulazeez Abubakar Kaka
and the Foundation delivered one of the most comprehensive grassroots
economic empowerment drives Kaduna North has ever seen — touching over
4,000 small businesses, entrepreneurs, and households across the state.


--------------------------------------------------------
  OUR APPROACH
--------------------------------------------------------

Economic empowerment under the Kowa Namu Ne philosophy is deliberate and
people-first. Every intervention is designed to create self-reliance —
not dependency. From cash grants to vocational training, from startup
capital to nano grants for market women, every initiative was aimed at
building lasting financial independence at the grassroots.


--------------------------------------------------------
  KEY ACTIVITIES & OUTCOMES
--------------------------------------------------------

LARGE-SCALE EMPOWERMENT — February 22nd
Over 1,300 beneficiaries received cash grants, working tools, and
entrepreneurial training across Kaduna North. The initiative targeted
small business owners and household breadwinners, equipping them with
the resources to stabilize and grow their livelihoods.

EMPOWERMENT 2.0 — August 17th
A second major empowerment wave reached an additional 1,300+
beneficiaries with financial support designed to expand income-generating
activities and reduce household poverty.

LARGE-SCALE COMMUNITY EMPOWERMENT — October 26th
Over 1,000 residents in the Kabala and Unguwan Shekara communities
received direct financial assistance to improve economic stability at
the grassroots level.

STARTUP CAPITAL FOR TEAM KAKA MEMBERS — June 29th & November 8th
Two batches of selected members received startup capital to fuel
entrepreneurship, build self-reliance, and create economic resilience
within the community network.

NANO GRANTS TO WOMEN TRADERS — October 12th
At the Arewa Vendors Association Event, women traders received targeted
nano grants to stabilize and expand their market businesses — empowering
them as economic contributors to their families and communities.

YOUNG ENTREPRENEURS SUMMIT SUPPORT — September 27th
Financial assistance was provided to small and medium enterprises (SMEs)
at the Young Entrepreneurs Summit, reinforcing a culture of innovation,
hustle, and homegrown enterprise.

SKILLS ACQUISITION GRADUATION — August 30th
Over 300 trainees successfully completed vocational skills programs
under the Kowa Namu Ne Foundation, marking a significant milestone in
human capital development. Graduates were equipped with practical,
market-relevant skills to generate sustainable income.

SOLAR EQUIPMENT DISTRIBUTION
Over 150 solar fans and electrical equipment were distributed to small
business owners, reducing operational costs and improving productivity
for micro-enterprises.

FOOD DISTRIBUTION — March 19th
Food items were distributed to over 500 low-income households, providing
timely relief and reinforcing social welfare support for vulnerable
families during periods of hardship.

WOMEN COOPERATIVE CONSULTATION — February 10th
A high-level strategic meeting was held with women leaders and
cooperative societies across communities, focused on building sustainable
economic support systems, increasing inclusion, and identifying new
pathways for women's financial empowerment.

WARD-LEVEL WOMEN FINANCIAL SUPPORT — April 3rd & 19th
Structured engagements were held with women across multiple wards,
accompanied by direct financial donations to strengthen women-led
business initiatives and grassroots cooperative organization.

  OUR COMMITMENT
--------------------------------------------------------

Because when one person rises economically, their family rises.

Kaduna rises.

Kowa Namu Ne — because leadership belongs to all of us.
