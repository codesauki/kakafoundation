OVERVIEW

2025 was a defining year in the journey of Hon. Abdulazeez Abubakar Kaka
— a year marked by action, inclusion, and purposeful leadership. Across
Kaduna North and beyond, Hon. Kaka consistently demonstrated that
leadership is not defined by position, but by service to the people.
From grassroots political engagements to large-scale empowerment
programs, education support, healthcare interventions, women and youth
inclusion, and strategic infrastructure projects, the year reflected a
clear commitment to sustainable development and human capital growth.

"Kaka in 2025" documents these efforts, not merely as a record of
activities, but as evidence of a people-first philosophy built on trust,
accessibility, and long-term vision. The initiatives captured in this
publication cut across political, social, economic, and humanitarian
sectors, reinforcing the belief that progress is achieved when
leadership listens, acts, and remains accountable to the people.

Together, we will continue to build a Kaduna, and a Nigeria that works
for all.

— Hon. Abdulazeez Abubakar Kaka

THE KOWA NAMU NE PHILOSOPHY

Kowa Namu Ne is more than a slogan; it is a philosophy of inclusive
leadership. It emphasizes shared ownership of governance, collective
responsibility, and equal opportunity for all, regardless of background,
gender, or social status.

Under this philosophy, leadership is participatory, empowerment is
deliberate, and development is sustainable. Every program implemented
reflects the belief that progress is strongest when the people are
involved — not as spectators, but as stakeholders.

The Kowa Namu Ne philosophy prioritizes:

-   Grassroots inclusion and consultation

-   Youth and women empowerment

-   Education and human capital development

-   Healthcare access and social welfare

-   Long-term leadership grooming

This philosophy continues to guide all interventions carried out under
Hon. Kaka's leadership and through the Kowa Namu Ne Foundation.

THE KOWA NAMU NE FOUNDATION

The Kowa Namu Ne Foundation serves as the humanitarian and development
arm of Hon. Abdulazeez Kaka's vision. Through the Foundation, structured
programs addressing education, healthcare, empowerment, and social
welfare are designed and implemented to reach the most vulnerable
members of society.

Key focus areas include:

-   Education for orphans and less privileged children

-   Skills acquisition and entrepreneurship support

-   Medical outreach and health interventions

-   Women and youth development initiatives

-   Community-based infrastructure projects

KAKA IN 2025 – ACTIVITIES HIGHLIGHTS

February 22nd – Large-Scale Empowerment of Beneficiaries

Over 1,300 beneficiaries received cash grants, working tools, and
entrepreneurial training, aimed at boosting small businesses and
improving household livelihoods.

March 18th – Youth Engagement Across Kaduna North

Hon. Kaka engaged youth groups to encourage political participation,
leadership consciousness, and involvement in entrepreneurship and
community development.

March 19th – Food Distribution to Vulnerable Families

Food items were distributed to over 500 low-income households, providing
timely relief and reinforcing social welfare support for vulnerable
families.

April 3rd & 19th – Ward-Level Women Engagements and Financial Support

Structured engagements were held with women across multiple wards,
accompanied by financial donations to strengthen women-led initiatives
and grassroots organization.

April 26th – Commissioning of Kowa Namu Ne Foundation School

A fully built, furnished, and maintained school for orphans and less
privileged children was commissioned, offering free Islamic and Western
education.

May 1st & 13th – Solar Installation at Masallacin Malam Goma and Shehu
Laminu Road, U/Rimi

Solar power was installed to improve lighting, safety, and access to
electricity for worshipers and surrounding residents. And a solar
project was delivered to enhance community safety, night-time
visibility, and general well-being.

June 15th – Free Medical Outreach in Malali

Residents benefitted from medical consultations, medications, and health
education through a free healthcare outreach.

June 29th – Startup Capital for Team Kaka Members (First Batch)

Selected members received startup capital to support entrepreneurship,
self-reliance, and economic stability.

July 6th – Engagement with Persons with Disabilities and Kauraye Groups

Hon. Kaka engaged marginalized groups and provided financial support to
promote inclusion, dignity, and economic participation.

July 28th – Health Insurance Sponsorship (KADCHMA)

Over 100 residents were enrolled into the state health insurance scheme,
expanding access to affordable healthcare.

July 29th – Support for Kadpoly Leadership Summit

A financial contribution was made to support youth leadership
development and intellectual engagement.

August 2nd – Medical Outreach and Eye Screening in Kabala Ward

Comprehensive medical services and eye screenings were provided, with
free eyeglasses distributed to beneficiaries.

August 3rd – Community Engagement in Unguwan Sarki

A townhall-style engagement was held with community members, alongside
financial support to address local needs.

August 17th – Empowerment 2.0 Initiative

Another phase of large-scale empowerment reached over 1,300
beneficiaries with financial support to expand livelihoods.

August 30th – Graduation of Skills Acquisition Trainees

Over 300 trainees successfully completed vocational programs, marking a
milestone in human capital development.

September 27th – Support to Small Business Owners at Young Entrepreneurs
Summit

Financial assistance was provided to SMEs, reinforcing support for
innovation and entrepreneurship.

September 29th – Distribution of Books and School Bags to Students

Educational materials were distributed to public schools to support
learning and reduce the burden on families.

September–November – Sponsorship of Online INEC Voter Registration

Thousands of residents were supported to register as voters,
strengthening democratic participation and civic inclusion.

October 12th – Nano Grants to Women at Arewa Vendors Association Event

Women traders received nano grants to stabilize and grow their
businesses.

October 12th – Donation During Reception of New APC Members

Financial support was extended to women and youth groups during the
reception of new party members.

October 13th – Settlement of Medical Bills at Primary Health Centres

Outstanding medical bills were settled to ensure patients received
timely and uninterrupted healthcare.

October 15th – Support to Kaduna North APC Executives

Hon. Kaka provided financial support following strategic engagements
aimed at strengthening party structure and unity.

October 26th – Large-Scale Community Empowerment in Kabala and Unguwan
Shekara

Over 1,000 beneficiaries received financial assistance to improve
economic stability at the grassroots.

November 6th – Installation of Solar Street Lights Across Multiple Wards

Solar streetlights were installed across communities, improving
security, mobility, and night-time activities.

November 8th – Second Batch Startup Capital for Team Kaka Members

Additional members received startup support to expand businesses and
sustain livelihoods.

November 11th & 12th – Back-to-School Support for Students

Uniforms, school bags, and writing materials were provided to support
children's return to school.

February 10th – Strategic Meeting with Women Groups and Cooperative
Societies

A high-level consultation with women leaders and cooperatives focused on
inclusion, economic empowerment, and creating sustainable support
systems for women across communities.
